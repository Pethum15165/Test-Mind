The 30-Day Money Reset

6 min read·All levels

📷 One month is all it takes to identify where your money really goes and build better habits

🔍 The Problem

Most people have no accurate idea where their money goes each month. They know their salary. They know their rent. Everything else is a blur. The 30-day reset is a one-month exercise that brings total financial clarity — and often reveals hundreds of dollars in completely unnoticed spending.

What the 30-Day Reset Is NOT
❌

It is not a permanent diet. You are not committing to living like this forever — just examining your habits clearly for one month.

❌

It is not punishment. No guilt, no judgment. You are simply gathering data about yourself to make better decisions.

The 30-Day Plan

The Money Reset

Four weeks of total financial awareness that changes how you think about money forever.

1

Week 1: Track every single purchase

Write it down — every coffee, every parking fee, every online order. No exceptions. Use your phone's notes app if that's easiest.

2

Week 2: Categorise and find the surprises

Group all spending: food, transport, entertainment, subscriptions, impulse. The surprises are usually in entertainment and subscriptions.

3

Week 3: Eliminate one full category

Pick the category that gives you the least joy per dollar — and cut it entirely for one week. Replace eating out with cooking. Cancel one streaming service.

4

Week 4: Rebuild with intention

Now you know what you actually value. Rebuild a spending plan that matches your real priorities — not what you defaulted into spending money on.

5

End of month: Make three decisions

What to cut permanently. What to keep. What to redirect into savings or debt repayment.

Track every purchase for the next 7 days

Just one week. No changes yet — just data. What you discover will surprise you. And it will change how you spend for the rest of the year.

⚡ Quick Summary

Track every purchase for 30 days — most people find $200–$500 in invisible spending

Categorise spending to see where money actually goes vs where you think it goes

Cut one full category for a week — clarity comes from the experiment

Rebuild your spending plan intentionally, based on what you actually value
